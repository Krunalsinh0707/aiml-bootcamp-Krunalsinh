The morning rain had stopped just before sunrise. Water still clung to
the leaves, and the streets reflected the pale orange light spreading
across the horizon. People emerged slowly from their homes, carrying
umbrellas that were no longer needed but felt reassuring to keep nearby.

At the corner café, the first customers gathered around small tables and
exchanged stories about the previous day’s events. The aroma of fresh
coffee drifted through the open doorway, mixing with the cool air.
Outside, bicycles rolled past, and the city began to settle into its
familiar rhythm.

By midday, the clouds had disappeared entirely. Children played in a
nearby park while office workers enjoyed a brief walk during their lunch
breaks. Although the day seemed ordinary, there was a quiet sense of
renewal that often follows a night of rain, as if the world had been
gently cleaned and given a fresh start.
